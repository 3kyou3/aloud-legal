Inner Dialogue — Privacy Policy

Effective July 17, 2026

Inner Dialogue is a private journal. Here, in plain language, is what the app collects, where it goes, and the controls you have. The short version: your writing is yours, nothing is sent to any AI until you ask for it, and we do not run ads, track you, or sell your data.

What the app stores, and where

When you sign in and use the app, the following are stored in our backend database — hosted by Supabase, in the United States — and tied to your account:

  • Your journal entries — the text you write or speak, whether each was written or spoken, when you wrote it, and (if you expanded on an earlier thought) the link between the two.
  • Reflections and your portrait — the text the AI writes about an entry, and the five-part "portrait" it draws from your writing. Created only when you ask (see The AI, and your consent).
  • Your name — if you provide it through Sign in with Apple, so the app can greet you. It is shown only to you.
  • Your subscription status — whether you have Inner Pro, so the app can unlock it. This is confirmed with Apple; we never see your payment details.
  • A count of reflections you generate — timestamps only, with none of your writing, used to enforce the free daily limit.
  • Feedback — only if you choose to send us a note from inside the app.

Every record is protected by database row-level security, which means it can be read only by your own account.

Sign in with Apple

The app signs you in with Apple. On your first sign-in, Apple may share your name and an email address (which can be Apple's private relay address). Your name is stored so the app can address you. Your email is stored by our authentication provider as your account identifier and is not read anywhere in the app. We never receive your Apple password.

On your device only

Some things never leave your phone:

  • Voice transcription — when you use Speak, your speech is transcribed on your device. The audio is never recorded, stored, or sent off your phone; only the text you keep becomes an entry.
  • Your unfinished draft — whatever is in the editor is saved locally so you do not lose it. It stays on the device.
  • App settings — your daily reminder and a cached copy of your name are stored locally on your device.

The AI, and your consent

Reflections and the portrait are generated by a third-party AI model — Google's Gemini — which we call from our own servers.

Nothing is ever sent to the AI until you ask for it. The first time you tap "Reflect on this," the app asks for your explicit permission, in plain words, and no writing leaves your phone until you agree. This is enforced on our servers, not just in the app.

  • Only the words of the entry are sent — and, if the entry was a reply to an earlier one, that earlier entry and its question, so the reflection makes sense. We do not send your name, your email, your account identifier, or any dates.
  • We use a paid Google API tier, under whose terms Google does not use your content to train its models. We do not use your writing to train any model.
  • You can turn the AI off at any time, which stops any further requests, and you can erase everything the AI has written about you (your reflections and portrait) without deleting your journal.

What we do not do

  • No advertising, and no ad networks.
  • No analytics or tracking that follows you across other apps or the web.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your writing with anyone, for any purpose.

Who processes data for us

  • Apple — Sign in with Apple, and subscription billing.
  • Supabase — hosts our database and authentication (United States).
  • Google — provides the AI model that writes reflections, receiving only entry text, and only after your consent.

Your controls

  • Turn the AI off — stops new reflections immediately.
  • Erase what the AI wrote — deletes your reflections and portrait; your entries stay.
  • Delete your account — permanently erases your account and everything tied to it (entries, reflections, portrait, subscription record, feedback) in one step, from inside the app. Deleting your account does not cancel an active Apple subscription — manage that in your device's Settings.

Data retention

We keep your data while your account exists. When you delete your account, it is removed. Deleting the app from your phone does not delete your account data — use "Delete account" in the app for that.

Security

Your data travels over encrypted connections (HTTPS) and is stored in a database where row-level security limits every record to its owner. No system is perfectly secure, but access to your writing is restricted to your own authenticated account.

Children

Inner Dialogue is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from them.

Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles your data, we will update this page and the effective date above.
